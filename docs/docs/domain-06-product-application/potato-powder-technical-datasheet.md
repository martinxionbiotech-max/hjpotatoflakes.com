# Hongji Potato Powder (60 Mesh) — Technical Datasheet

## Product Overview

Hongji Agriculture's **Potato Powder (60 Mesh)** is a finely milled dehydrated potato product produced from high-solids processing potato varieties including Atlantic, Shepody, and Russet Burbank. Unlike Potato Flakes (which retain a flake-like physical structure), Potato Powder is milled through a 60-mesh (250 μm) screen, resulting in a free-flowing fine powder with rapid hydration, uniform particle size distribution, and excellent blending properties.

Potato Powder is the preferred ingredient for dry-mix applications (instant soup bases, seasoning blends, bakery premixes), extruded snacks, batter systems, meat binders, and nutritional supplements where uniform dispersion and rapid solubility are critical. The finer particle size provides faster hydration kinetics and smoother finished texture compared to standard flakes, making it the ingredient of choice for applications requiring seamless integration into dry blends and batters.

## 2. Manufacturing Process

### 2.1 Process Flow

```
Raw Potatoes → Washing & Destoning → Steam Peeling → Inspection & Trimming →
Slicing (8–12 mm) → Blanching (70°C, 12 min) → Steam Cooking (100°C, 30 min) →
Mashing → Drum Drying → Flaking → Pre-crushing → Hammer Milling →
Air Classification → Sieving (60 Mesh) → Metal Detection → Packaging
```

### 2.2 Detailed Process Parameters

| Process Step | Parameters | Purpose |
|-------------|-----------|---------|
| **Washing & destoning** | Rotating drum washer, 20 m³/h water flow | Remove soil, debris, stones |
| **Steam peeling** | 12–15 bar, 20–30 seconds | Rapid skin removal, minimal cook loss |
| **Inspection** | Manual sorting on conveyor belt | Remove defective tubers and peel remnants |
| **Slicing** | Slices 8–12 mm thickness | Uniform cooking surface area |
| **Blanching** | Hot water, 70°C, 12 minutes | Enzyme inactivation (polyphenol oxidase, peroxidase), leaching of reducing sugars |
| **Steam cooking** | Atmospheric steam, 98–100°C, 25–35 minutes | Complete starch gelatinization, tissue softening |
| **Mashing** | Rotary masher or roller mill, ≤ 3 mm gap | Homogeneous mash without excessive cell rupture |
| **Drum drying** | Single drum, 160–175°C surface, 15–25 sec residence | Rapid dehydration to ≤ 6.0% moisture |
| **Flaking** | Doctor blade, flake breaker | Separate dried sheet into manageable flakes |
| **Pre-crushing** | Knife mill, 5 mm screen | Reduce flake size for hammer mill feed |
| **Hammer milling** | 3,000–4,000 RPM, 250 μm screen | Grind to target particle size |
| **Air classification** | Cut point: 250 μm | Remove ultra-fine fraction (< 75 μm) if needed |
| **Sieving** | 60 mesh (250 μm) stainless steel, vibratory | Ensure ≥ 95% passing 60 mesh |
| **Metal detection** | Fe ≥ 0.5 mm, SUS ≥ 1.0 mm, Non-Fe ≥ 0.8 mm | Contaminant rejection with auto-ejection |

> **Critical Quality Point — Hammer Mill Speed:** The hammer mill speed of 3,000–4,000 RPM is optimized to produce the target particle distribution. Speeds above 4,500 RPM generate excessive fines (< 75 μm), which increase dust generation and hygroscopicity. Speeds below 2,500 RPM yield insufficient throughput and a coarser fraction requiring recirculation.

## 12. Storage & Shelf Life

| Condition | Specification | Notes |
|-----------|-------------|-------|
| **Shelf life (unopened, standard packaging)** | 24 months from production date | At optimal conditions |
| **Shelf life (vacuum-packed)** | 36 months from production date | Reduced oxidation, no moisture ingress |
| **Storage temperature** | ≤ 25°C (optimal: 15–20°C) | Lower temperatures extend shelf life |
| **Relative humidity** | ≤ 60% | Powder is more hygroscopic than flakes |
| **Pallet stacking max.** | 4 pallets high | 5+ pallets risks compression and caking |
| **After opening** | Reseal immediately, use within 2 weeks | Moisture pickup accelerates rapidly |
| **Avoid** | Direct sunlight, moisture ingress, temperature fluctuations | Cycling > ±5°C/day degrades quality |
| **Odor absorption** | Store away from strong odors | High surface area absorbs volatile compounds |

> **⚠ Important:** Due to its higher surface area-to-volume ratio, Potato Powder is significantly more hygroscopic than Potato Flakes. Once a bag is opened, it must be resealed tightly and stored in a dry environment (≤ 60% RH). In high-humidity conditions (> 70% RH), powder can begin caking within 24–48 hours of exposure.

### 12.1 Accelerated Shelf Life Data

| Storage Condition | Time to Quality Limit | Key Failure Mode |
|------------------|----------------------|------------------|
| 25°C / 60% RH | 24 months | Color darkening (ΔE > 3.0) |
| 30°C / 70% RH | 10 months | Caking, moisture > 6.5%, flow reduction |
| 40°C / 80% RH | 5 months | Browning (Maillard), off-flavor development |
| −18°C (frozen) | 60+ months | Excellent stability, no significant change |
