## 3. Experimental Design

### 3.1 Experimental Objective

Develop three flavored snack powder seasoning products (BBQ, Tomato, Cheese) using the potato carrier blend (C), optimize the carrier-to-seasoning ratio, and validate flavor stability over 30 days of accelerated storage.

### 3.2 Factor-Level Design

Three separate factor experiments were conducted — one for each flavor profile.

#### Factor Design for BBQ Flavor

| Factor | Level 1 | Level 2 | Level 3 | Level 4 |
|:---|:---:|:---:|:---:|:---:|
| Carrier base (%) | 60% | 65% | 70% | 75% |
| BBQ seasoning blend (%) | 40% | 35% | 30% | 25% |
| Spray oil post-treatment (%) | 8% | 10% | 12% | — |

#### Factor Design for Tomato Flavor

|:---|:---:|:---:|:---:|:---:|

#### Factor Design for Cheese Flavor

| Factor | Level 1 | Level 2 | Level 3 | Level 4 |
|:---|:---:|:---:|:---:|:---:|

| Cheese seasoning blend (%) | 45% | 40% | 35% | 30% |
| Spray oil post-treatment (%) | 8% | 10% | 12% | — |

### 3.3 Processing Protocol

The flavored snack powders were prepared using the following standardized procedure:

1. **Dry blending:** Potato carrier base + seasoning powder blend in ribbon blender (200 kg batch)
2. **Blending conditions:** 15 min at 25 rpm, room temperature (22–25°C), <60% RH
3. **Spray oil application:** Refined sunflower oil sprayed via twin-fluid nozzle at 3 bar atomizing pressure
4. **Post-oil blending:** Continue blending for 8 min after oil addition
5. **Screening:** Pass through 500 µm vibratory sieve to break agglomerates
6. **Packaging:** Sealed in 4-ply aluminum foil bags (5 kg), flushed with nitrogen
7. **Accelerated storage:** 30 days at 38°C / 70% RH (equivalent to ~12 months ambient)

### 3.4 Replication

Each flavor × carrier ratio combination was prepared in duplicate. Each analytical test was performed in triplicate. Sensory testing was conducted with 40 panelists (typical consumers of savory snacks in the target region).

**Observation:** All three variants showed excellent coating adhesion (>75% on Day 0), with minimal loss (<3%) over 30 days of accelerated storage. The BBQ variant with higher carrier ratio (70%) showed the best adhesion profile.

## 5. Results & Analysis

### 5.1 Carrier Ratio Optimization

The optimal carrier-to-seasoning ratio differed by flavor variant due to the different physical properties of the flavor ingredients:

| Flavor | Optimal Carrier Ratio | Rationale |
|:---|---|:---|
| BBQ | 70% | Higher smoke/heat content required more dilution to prevent clumping |
| Tomato | 65% | Tomato powder is acidic and requires adequate carrier to buffer sensory impact |
| Cheese | 60% | Cheese powder is lipophilic and adheres well even at lower carrier levels; higher seasoning loading provides maximum cheese impact |

### 5.2 Effect of Spray Oil on Coating Adhesion

Spray oil level had a significant impact on coating adhesion across all three flavors (ANOVA, p < 0.01):

| Spray Oil Level | BBQ Adhesion | Tomato Adhesion | Cheese Adhesion |
|:---|---:|---:|---:|
| 8% | 75.2% | 72.1% | 69.8% |
| 10% | 82.3% | 78.5% | 76.8% |
| 12% | 83.1% | 79.2% | 77.5% |

There was no statistically significant difference between 10% and 12% spray oil (p > 0.05), so 10% was selected as the standard to minimize fat content.

### 5.3 Flavor Stability Mechanism

The potato flour/flakes blend provided excellent flavor protection through several mechanisms:

1. **Microencapsulation effect:** Starch granules in the potato carrier absorb and physically trap volatile flavor compounds, reducing oxidation and evaporation during storage.
2. **Water activity control:** The low aw (0.38) of the potato blend inhibits Maillard browning reactions and lipid oxidation in the seasoning components.
3. **Oil absorption:** The porous structure of potato flakes absorbs the spray oil, forming a protective lipid matrix around flavor compounds.

### 5.4 Particle Size and Free-Flow Properties

| Property | BBQ | Tomato | Cheese | Specification |
|:---|---:|---:|---:|:---|
| d10 (µm) | 62 | 58 | 55 | >50 µm |
| d50 (µm) | 195 | 185 | 178 | 150–250 µm |
| d90 (µm) | 410 | 395 | 388 | <500 µm |
| Angle of repose | 37° | 39° | 40° | <42° |
| Caking index (after 30 days) | 12.5 | 14.2 | 15.8 | <20% |

All variants met the free-flow specification with angle of repose <42°, indicating good flowability for automated seasoning application systems.
