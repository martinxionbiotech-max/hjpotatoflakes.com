# Application Guide: Potato Flakes & Powder in Soups, Sauces & Prepared Meals

## Scope & Objective

This technical guide provides comprehensive formulation and processing parameters for incorporating Hongji Potato Flakes and Potato Powder into soups, sauces, gravies, and prepared meal applications. The guide is intended for R&D teams and production managers in the savory food manufacturing sector, covering instant dry mix formulations, wet-process sauces and soups, retorted products, frozen prepared meals, and culinary bases.

Potato-derived ingredients offer unique advantages in savory liquid and semi-liquid systems: high cold-water solubility (especially powder), exceptional water binding, smooth mouthfeel, clean flavor profile, and freeze-thaw stability. They function both as a thickener and as a flavor carrier, enabling reduced usage of modified starches and gums.

---

## 1. Product Selection for Soups & Sauces

### 1.1 Choosing Between Flakes and Powder

| Application | Recommended Product | Rationale |
|------------|-------------------|-----------|
| **Instant dry soup mixes** | Potato Powder (60 Mesh) | Rapid cold/hot hydration, fine dispersion |
| **Retort / canned soups** | Potato Flakes | Heat-stable thickener, won't break down |
| **Frozen soups / sauces** | Potato Powder | Freeze-thaw stability, smooth texture |
| **Gravy mixes (dry)** | Potato Powder | Disperses without clumping |
| **Wet sauces (hot-fill)** | Potato Flakes | Viscosity builder, smooth mouthfeel |
| **Cheese sauces** | Potato Powder | Avoids graininess, melts smoothly |
| **Prepared meal components** | Both (blend optimized) | Balanced function |

### 1.2 Key Functional Properties for Liquid Systems

| Property | Potato Flakes | Potato Powder (60 Mesh) |
|----------|-------------|------------------------|
| Cold water hydration | Partial (15–25%) | Good (20–40%) |
| Hot water viscosity | 2,000–3,500 cP (peak) | 1,500–2,800 cP (peak) |
| Clumping tendency | Low-moderate | Low (free-flowing) |
| Stability in acid (pH < 4.0) | Moderate | Good |
| Stability in high salt (>3%) | Good | Good |
| Freeze-thaw stability | Good (≥ 3 cycles) | Very good (≥ 5 cycles) |
| Clarity in solution | Opaque, creamy | Opaque, creamy |
| Flavor contribution | Mild potato | Very mild |

### 2.2 Cream of Potato Soup — Wet Process (Canned / Retort)

| Ingredient | Proportion (%) |
|-----------|--------------|
| **Potato Flakes (HJ-PF-01)** | **4.5** |
| Water | 72.0 |

| Sugar | 0.5 |

| White pepper | 0.1 |
| Nutmeg | 0.02 |
| Xanthan gum | 0.08 |

**Process:**
1. Sauté onion and garlic in butter (3 min, 90°C)
2. Add water and milk; heat to 80°C
3. Slowly sprinkle Potato Flakes while whisking
4. Add modified starch slurry (1:3 in cold water)
5. Add cream, salt, sugar, spices
6. Hold at 85°C for 5 min (starch cook)
7. Hot fill at ≥ 80°C into cans/retort pouches
8. Retort at 121°C for 25–35 min (F₀ ≥ 6)
9. Final viscosity target: 2,000–4,000 cP (Brookfield, 25°C, spindle #3)

**Frozen Meal Process:**
1. Prepare meat filling; cool to 25°C
2. Reconstitute Potato Flakes at 1:3.5 (firm mash)
3. Fold in butter, milk powder, egg yolk, seasonings
4. Fill tray: 150 g filling + 100 g potato topping

7. Reheat: 180°C oven for 30 min or microwave (750W, 5 min)

## 10. Troubleshooting Guide

| Problem | Cause | Solution |
|---------|-------|----------|
| **Lumpy sauce/soup** | Potato ingredient added too fast to hot liquid | Pre-disperse in cold water or pre-blend with dry ingredients |
| **Thin / low viscosity** | Insufficient potato content | Increase by 0.5–1.0%; check if modified starch needed |
| | Under-cooked starch system | Increase hold time at 85–90°C |
| **Grainy texture** | Over-cooking / shear degradation | Reduce shear; add near end of process |
| **Pasty / gluey** | Over-gelatinization | Reduce temperature; reduce mixing time |
| | Wrong product (too fine) | Use Standard Flakes instead of Powder |
| **Off-flavor** | Rancidity in fat component | Check fat sources; add antioxidant (tocopherol) |
| | Stale potato ingredient | Check flake production date |
| **Color too dark** | Maillard reaction (high temp, reduction) | Reduce processing temperature; check reducing sugars |
| | Caramelization of sugars | Add SAPP or citric acid |
| **Syneresis / water separation** | Starch retrogradation | Add xanthan gum (0.05–0.1%); use cross-linked starch |
| **Mold / spoilage (short shelf life)** | High water activity; poor hot-fill temp | Ensure fill temperature ≥ 80°C; target aw ≤ 0.95 |
