# Application Guide: Potato Flakes & Powder in Snacks & Extrusion

## Scope & Objective

This technical guide provides comprehensive formulation and processing parameters for using Hongji Potato Flakes and Potato Powder in snack food manufacturing, with a focus on direct-expanded (extruded) snacks, formed pellets, fabricated chips, and coated snack applications. The document is intended for R&D teams, process engineers, and production managers in the snack food industry who seek to optimize texture, expansion ratio, flavor delivery, and production efficiency through potato-based ingredient incorporation.

Potato-derived ingredients are fundamental to the snack industry. The pre-gelatinized starch in Potato Flakes and the fine, uniform particle size of Potato Powder (60 Mesh) offer distinct advantages in different snack processing platforms.

## 2. Direct-Expanded Extruded Snacks

### 2.1 Functional Role of Potato Ingredients

In direct-expanded (direct expansion) extrusion, the potato ingredient serves three critical roles:
1. **Starch gelatinization matrix** — Provides the structural network for bubble formation and expansion
2. **Water binding** — Controls moisture delivery to the extrusion barrel
3. **Flavor base** — Delivers authentic potato flavor and browning potential

### 2.3 Extrusion Parameters

| Parameter | Potato Powder (30–40%) | Potato Flakes (15–25%) |
|-----------|----------------------|----------------------|
| **Extruder type** | Twin-screw preferred | Twin-screw required |
| **Barrel temperature profile** | Zone 1: 80–100°C | Zone 1: 70–90°C |
| | Zone 2: 120–140°C | Zone 2: 110–130°C |
| | Zone 3: 140–165°C | Zone 3: 130–155°C |
| | Die: 150–170°C | Die: 140–160°C |
| **Feed moisture** | 14 – 18% | 16 – 22% |
| **Screw speed** | 300 – 500 RPM | 250 – 400 RPM |
| **Feed rate** | 20 – 40 kg/h (per 25 mm barrel) | 18 – 35 kg/h |
| **Die pressure** | 40 – 80 bar | 35 – 70 bar |
| **Die diameter** | 2 – 6 mm (round) | 2 – 8 mm |
| **Expansion ratio (SME dependent)** | 3:1 to 5:1 | 2.5:1 to 4:1 |

### 2.4 Effect of Potato Inclusion on Extrusion Performance

| Parameter | 0% Potato | 20% Potato Powder | 35% Potato Powder | 50% Potato Powder |
|-----------|----------|------------------|------------------|------------------|
| **Expansion ratio** | 4.8:1 | 4.5:1 (−6%) | 4.0:1 (−17%) | 3.2:1 (−33%) |
| **Bulk density (g/L)** | 55 | 65 (+18%) | 80 (+45%) | 110 (+100%) |
| **Piece hardness (N)** | 8.5 | 9.8 (+15%) | 11.5 (+35%) | 14.0 (+65%) |
| **Crispness (acoustic)** | Excellent | Very Good | Good | Moderate |
| **Oil absorption (%)** | 22 | 24 | 27 | 32 |

> **Note:** Higher potato inclusion increases bulk density and hardness while reducing expansion ratio. For maximum expansion (collets), keep potato inclusion ≤ 25%. For a dense, crunchy texture (snack pellets), 35–50% is preferred.

### 5.1 Potato Powder as Seasoning Carrier

- Neutral flavor profile
- High oil absorption (1.5–2.5 g/g)

## 7. Troubleshooting Snack Extrusion

| Problem | Root Cause | Solution |
|---------|-----------|----------|
| **Poor expansion** | Insufficient starch gelatinization | Increase barrel temperature by 5–10°C; reduce feed rate |
| | Moisture too high | Reduce feed moisture by 1–2% |
| | Potato inclusion too high | Reduce to ≤ 30% or add expandable starch |
| **Uneven texture** | Poor mixing | Pre-blend dry ingredients for 5 min; check mixer |
| | Particle size variation | Ensure consistent grinding (uniform mesh) |
| **Dark color / scorching** | Excessive reducing sugars | Switch to low-sugar variety or increase moisture |
| | Hot spots in barrel | Check barrel temperature uniformity; clean deposits |
| **High oil pickup** | Low expansion → dense piece | Optimize expansion as above |
| | High potato content (>35%) | Reduce potato; increase oil-resistant starch |
| **Sticky dough** | Over-gelatinization | Reduce barrel temp; add ungelatinized starch |
| **Blistering / surface defects** | Moisture differential | Equalize moisture distribution; temper longer |
