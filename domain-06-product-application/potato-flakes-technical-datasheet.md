# Hongji Potato Flakes — Technical Datasheet

## Product Overview

Hongji Agriculture's **Potato Flakes** (also known as Potato Granules or Instant Mashed Potato Flakes) are dehydrated potato products manufactured from premium processing potato varieties grown in the Zhangjiakou region of Hebei Province, China. The product is produced through a fully integrated supply chain spanning from proprietary seed breeding to controlled field cultivation and advanced processing, ensuring traceability, consistency, and food safety from farm to finished ingredient.

Potato Flakes serve as a critical ingredient in the global food manufacturing industry, valued for their superior water absorption, texture contribution, and clean-label appeal. They are widely used in bakery products, extruded snacks, instant mashed potatoes, soups, sauces, prepared meals, and meat products. The drum-drying process used to produce Hongji Potato Flakes preserves the natural potato cell structure, resulting in a product that reconstitutes into a mash with a texture closely resembling fresh-cooked potatoes.

## 2. Manufacturing Process

```
Seed Breeding → Field Cultivation → Harvest → Washing & Destoning → 
Steam Peeling → Inspection & Trimming → Slicing (8–12 mm) → 
Pre-Cooking (68–72°C) → Steam Cooking (98–100°C) → Mashing →
Drum Drying → Flaking → Milling (optional) → Screening → 
Metal Detection → Packaging → Storage (≤25°C, ≤65% RH)
```

### 2.1 Detailed Process Parameters

| Process Step | Parameters | Purpose |
|-------------|-----------|---------|
| **Washing & destoning** | Water flow 20 m³/h, rotating drum washer | Remove soil, stones, debris |
| **Steam peeling** | 12–15 bar, 20–30 seconds | Rapid skin removal, minimal cook loss |
| **Pre-cooking** | 68–72°C, 15–20 minutes | Starch gelatinization initiation, enzyme inactivation |
| **Steam cooking** | 98–100°C, 25–35 minutes | Complete gelatinization, cell wall softening |
| **Mashing** | Rotary masher, ≤ 3 mm gap | Break cooked potatoes into fine mash without cell rupture |
| **Drum drying** | Single drum, 155–175°C surface, 15–25 sec residence | Rapid dehydration, thin film formation |
| **Flaking** | Doctor blade removal, flake breaker | Separate dried sheet into flakes |
| **Screening** | Vibrating sieve, 6 mesh (optional mill through 20 mesh) | Remove oversize particles |
| **Metal detection** | Fe ≥ 0.5 mm, SUS ≥ 1.0 mm, Non-Fe ≥ 0.8 mm | Contaminant rejection |

> **Critical Quality Step — Pre-cooking Temperature Control:** The pre-cooking stage at 68–72°C is precisely controlled to initiate controlled starch gelatinization. This step is essential for modifying the rheological properties of the final mash. If pre-cooking temperature exceeds 75°C, excessive gelatinization causes sticky, pasty rehydrated mash. If below 65°C, insufficient gelatinization results in a gritty texture.

## 11. Storage & Shelf Life

| Condition | Specification | Notes |
|-----------|-------------|-------|
| **Shelf life (unopened, standard packaging)** | 24 months from production date | At optimal conditions |
| **Shelf life (vacuum/N₂ flushed packaging)** | 36 months from production date | Reduced oxidation |
| **Storage temperature** | ≤ 25°C (optimal: 15–20°C) | Lower = better quality retention |
| **Relative humidity** | ≤ 65% | Above 70% RH accelerates caking |
| **Pallet stacking max.** | 4 pallets high | 5+ pallets risks compression damage |
| **After opening** | Reseal tightly, use within 4 weeks | Moisture pickup increases rapidly |
| **Avoid** | Direct sunlight, condensation, temperature cycling | Cycling > ±5°C/day degrades quality |
| **Odor sensitivity** | Store away from strong-smelling materials (spices, chemicals, garlic) | Potato flakes readily absorb ambient odors |
| **Best practice** | FIFO (First-In, First-Out) rotation | Date-stamped on every bag |
