## 1. Introduction

International trade in potato flakes and potato powder requires compliance with multiple regulatory frameworks. Buyers in different markets reference distinct standards for food safety, quality parameters, labeling, and permissible additives. This document provides a comprehensive comparison of the major standards systems governing dehydrated potato products:

| Jurisdiction | Standards Body | Governing Regulation |
|--------------|---------------|---------------------|
| **China** | SAC / NHFPC | GB (National Standards), SB/T (Trade Standards) |
| **European Union** | EC / EFSA | EU Regulations 231/2012, 1169/2011, 1881/2006 |
| **United States** | FDA / USDA | 21 CFR Parts 101, 110, 165; GRAS notices |
| **Codex Alimentarius** | FAO/WHO Joint Program | Codex Standard 214-1999 (revised) |

## 3. Additive and Processing Aid Regulations

### 3.1 Permitted Additives for Dehydrated Potato Products

| Additive | China (GB 2760-2014) | EU (231/2012) | US (21 CFR) | Codex (CXS 192-1995) |
|----------|:--------------------:|:-------------:|:-----------:|:---------------------:|
| **Sodium sulfite** (preservative) | ✓ (max 50 mg/kg as SO₂) | ✓ (max 50 mg/kg as SO₂) | ✓ (GRAS, max 50 mg/kg) | ✓ (max 50 mg/kg) |
| **Potassium metabisulfite** | ✓ (max 50 mg/kg as SO₂) | ✓ (max 50 mg/kg as SO₂) | ✓ (GRAS, max 50 mg/kg) | ✓ (max 50 mg/kg) |
| **Ascorbic acid (Vitamin C)** | ✓ (QS) | ✓ (QS) | ✓ (GRAS) | ✓ (QS) |
| **Citric acid** | ✓ (QS) | ✓ (QS) | ✓ (GRAS) | ✓ (QS) |
| **Sodium acid pyrophosphate (SAPP)** | ✓ (max 2,000 mg/kg) | ✓ (max 2,500 mg/kg) | ✓ (GRAS) | ✓ (max 2,500 mg/kg) |
| **Calcium propionate** | ✓ (max 2,500 mg/kg) | ✓ (max 2,000 mg/kg) | ✓ (GRAS) | ✓ (max 2,000 mg/kg) |
| **TBHQ (antioxidant)** | ✓ (max 200 mg/kg) | ✗ (not permitted) | ✓ (max 200 mg/kg) | ✓ (max 200 mg/kg) |
| **BHA/BHT** | ✓ (max 200 mg/kg) | ✗ (not permitted) | ✓ (max 200 mg/kg) | ✓ (max 200 mg/kg) |
| **Mono/diglycerides** | ✓ (QS) | ✓ (QS) | ✓ (GRAS) | ✓ (QS) |

✓ = Permitted | ✗ = Not permitted | QS = Quantity sufficient, subject to GMP

> **Important Note:** The EU maintains a notably stricter policy regarding synthetic antioxidants (TBHQ, BHA, BHT), which are not permitted in dehydrated potatoes. Products destined for the European market must use natural alternatives or adjust formulation accordingly.

## 5. Sampling, Testing, and Inspection Protocols

### 5.1 Standard Sampling Plans

| Standard | Sampling Method | Lot Acceptance | Notable Difference |
|----------|----------------|:--------------:|-------------------|
| **GB/T 31736-2015** | Random sampling per lot; minimum 1 kg sample | AQL 2.5 (normal) | Allows batch composite testing |
| **Codex CXS 214-1999** | ISO 2859 (AQL-based) | AQL 1.0 (normal) | Stricter AQL on defectives |
| **EU Regulation** | Regulation (EC) 882/2004; official control sampling | Risk-based, varies | Heavy emphasis on HACCP verification |
| **US FDA** | 21 CFR 110.35; FDA Compliance Program | FDA Risk-based PAT | Focuses on CGMP compliance, not lot-by-lot |

### 5.2 CIQ Export Inspection (China)

For export of potato flakes from China:

1. **Pre-export inspection** by local CIQ office
2. **Sample analysis** — full parameter testing at CNAS-accredited lab
3. **Health Certificate** issuance (Chinese + English bilingual format)
4. **Phytosanitary Certificate** from General Administration of Customs (GAC)
5. **Certificate of Origin** (for preferential tariff treatment under Free Trade Agreements)

## 7. Convergence Efforts and Future Trends

International harmonization of potato product standards is progressing through several channels:

1. **Codex Alimentarius revisions** — CXS 214-1999 is under periodic review to reduce divergence between national standards
2. **China's GB standards modernization** — The 2023–2025 revision cycle for GB/T 31736 aims to align microbiological limits with Codex/EU norms
3. **Mutual recognition agreements** — China-EU Geographical Indications agreement (2021) and potential future food safety MRAs
4. **GFSI benchmarked schemes** — Increasing adoption of BRCGS, FSSC 22000, and SQF by Chinese exporters to bridge gap with international customer expectations
