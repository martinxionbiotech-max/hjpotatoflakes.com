## 1. Introduction

Exporting potato flakes and potato powder from China to international markets requires a comprehensive set of trade documents. Missing or incorrect documentation is the single most common cause of customs delays, cargo holds, and financial penalties. This guide covers the complete documentation chain required by **Standard Export Procedure from China** and typical documentation requested by international buyers across different markets.

## 3. Detailed Document Requirements

### 3.1 Commercial Invoice

The commercial invoice is the central document for customs valuation. Key requirements:

| Field | Requirement | Notes |
|-------|-------------|-------|
| Shipper (exporter) | Full legal name, address, tax ID | Must match registered exporter in GAC system |
| Consignee | Full legal name, address | May differ from notify party (in B/L) |
| Product description | Full HS Code and commercial description | HS 0712.90.9000 (dehydrated vegetables) |
| Quantity | Net and gross weight (kg), number of packages | |
| Unit price & total value | USD or EUR; Incoterms specification | e.g., "CIF Rotterdam" or "FOB Tianjin" |
| Payment terms | e.g., "T/T 30% deposit, 70% against B/L copy" | |
| Country of origin | "China" | |
| Port of loading / discharge | e.g., "Tianjin, China → Jebel Ali, UAE" | |
| Marks and numbers | As per packing list and B/L | |
| Invoice number and date | Unique per shipment | |

### 3.2 Packing List

Provides detailed breakdown of cargo contents:

| Item | Specification |
|------|-------------|
| Number of packages | Total cartons, bags, drums, or pallets |
| Net weight per package | e.g., 25 kg net/bag |
| Gross weight per package | Includes packaging weight |
| Dimensions per package | e.g., 40 × 30 × 20 cm |
| Total net weight | Sum of all packages |
| Total gross weight | Sum including all packaging materials |
| Pallet information | Number of pallets, pallet dimensions |
| Container information | Container number, seal number, container tare weight |

### 3.3 Bill of Lading (B/L)

Three main types used in potato flake trade:

| Type | Description | When Used |
|------|-------------|-----------|
| Original B/L (negotiable) | "To Order" or "To Order of Shipper"; endorsed to consignee | L/C transactions; buyer needs original for cargo release |
| Sea Waybill (non-negotiable) | Straight consignment to named consignee | Prepaid shipments; trusted buyer relationships |
| Telex Release | Electronic release without original B/L | Fast shipment; buyer requires no original documents |

**Typical B/L details for potato flake shipments:**

- Container type: 20' GP (typically 26–28 MT loading for flakes)
- B/L weight: gross weight of all cargo incl. packing
- Shipped on board date: critical for L/C compliance
- Place of receipt: Tianjin, Qingdao, or Shanghai (primary China ports for potato products)

### 3.4 Certificate of Origin (CO)

| Certificate Type | Administered By | Purpose |
|-----------------|-----------------|---------|
| **GSP Form A** | CIQ (China Customs) | Generalized System of Preferences — preferential duty rates for developing countries |
| **Form B** (ASEAN-China FTA) | CIQ | Duty preference for ASEAN member states |
| **Form E** (ASEAN-China FTA v2) | CIQ | Updated ASEAN-China FTA certificate |
| **Form F** (China-Chile FTA) | CIQ | Duty preference for exports to Chile |
| **Form X** (China-Switzerland FTA) | CIQ | Duty preference for exports to Switzerland |
| **Form P** (China-Pakistan FTA) | CIQ | Duty preference for exports to Pakistan |
| **Form M** (China-New Zealand FTA) | CIQ | Duty preference for exports to New Zealand |
| **COO (non-preferential)** | China Chamber of Commerce (CCPIT) | Generic certificate for countries without FTA |

### 3.5 CIQ Health Certificate (卫生证书 / Inspection Certificate)

Issued by the General Administration of Customs of China (GAC, formerly CIQ) at the port of export.

**Required tests typically include:**

- Organoleptic inspection (color, odor, texture, foreign matter)
- Moisture content determination
- Heavy metals screening (Pb, As, Cd, Hg)
- Microbiological analysis (Salmonella, E. coli, APC, yeast & mold)
- Sulfur dioxide (SO₂) content
- Pesticide residue screening (if required by destination market)

**Validity:** Typically 7–15 days from the date of issue. The certificate covers a specific lot/batch.

### 3.6 Phytosanitary Certificate

Mandatory for nearly all plant-based product exports to prevent the spread of quarantine pests. Issued by GAC/Plant Quarantine Office.

**Key declarations for potato flakes:**

- Product has been processed (heat-dried) to eliminate pest risk
- Free from quarantine pests listed by the importing country
- No live insects, mites, or fungal pathogens detected
- Packing materials are pest-free (if applicable)

**Countries with specific phytosanitary requirements for Chinese potato products:**

| Country | Additional Requirement | Reference |
|---------|----------------------|-----------|
| Australia | BIP (Biosecurity Import Permit) + treatment certification | BICON import conditions |
| South Korea | Additional WPM (Wood Packaging Material) inspection | Korean Plant Quarantine |
| Japan | Japanese Plant Protection Station inspection at arrival | Plant Protection Law |
| United States | APHIS PPQ Form 203; treatments per 7 CFR 319 | USDA APHIS regulations |
| EU countries | Plant passport equivalency for processed products | EU Plant Health Regulation 2016/2031 |
| Saudi Arabia | SASO-certified lab testing for aflatoxins, pesticides | SASO standards |
| Brazil | MAPA registration + phytosanitary certification | IN 39/2017 |

### 3.7 Certificate of Analysis (CoA)

Issued by the manufacturer's QA department (or third-party lab) confirming the product meets specifications.

**Standard CoA parameters for potato flakes:**

1. **Physical:** Moisture, bulk density, particle size distribution, color (Hunter L/a/b)
2. **Chemical:** Protein, starch, ash, SO₂, reducing sugars, total sugars, pH (reconstituted)
3. **Microbiological:** APC, coliforms, E. coli, Salmonella, yeast & mold, B. cereus
4. **Extraneous matter:** Dark specks count, foreign matter

### 3.8 Fumigation Certificate

Required by most countries when wood packaging materials (pallets, dunnage) are used. Must comply with **ISPM 15** (International Standards for Phytosanitary Measures No. 15).

- Treatment: Heat treatment (HT) to 56°C core for 30 minutes, or Methyl Bromide fumigation (MB)
- Marking: IPPC logo + country code + treatment facility code + treatment type (HT/MB)

> **Recommendation:** Use heat-treated (HT) pallets for European and US markets. Methyl bromide (MB) is being phased out in many jurisdictions.

### 3.9 Certificate of Free Sale (CFS)

For food products exported to certain markets (primarily Middle East, Africa, Latin America):

- Certified by China Chamber of Commerce for Import & Export of Foodstuffs (CCCFNA) or equivalent
- Declares the product is legally sold and freely available in the domestic Chinese market
- Valid for 1–2 years from date of issue

## 4. Letter of Credit (L/C) Compliance

### 4.1 Most Common L/C Types for Potato Flake Trade

| Type | Description | Risk Level for Exporter |
|------|-------------|:----------------------:|
| Irrevocable, Confirmed L/C | Adding bank confirmation; payment guaranteed by confirming bank | Low |
| Irrevocable, Unconfirmed L/C | Payment obligation from issuing bank only | Moderate |
| Sight L/C | Payment upon presentation of compliant documents | Low |
| Usance (Time) L/C | Payment at 30, 60, 90 days after B/L date | Moderate |
| Revolving L/C | Multiple shipments under a single L/C structure | Low |

### 4.2 Critical L/C Compliance Checklist for Potato Flake Shipments

| Item | Rule | Common Discrepancy |
|------|------|--------------------|
| **Latest shipment date** | Must ship on or before date; never after | Late shipment = discrepancy |
| **L/C expiry** | Documents must be presented by expiry date | Late presentation = discrepancy |
| **B/L presentation** | Typically within 21 days from B/L date | Backdated / stale B/L not accepted |
| **Product description** | Must match L/C exactly; no abbreviation unless specified | Partial or incorrect description |
| **Weight tolerance** | Typically ±5% unless specified otherwise | Over/under shipment tolerance exceeded |
| **Non-negotiable docs** | All copies as per L/C checklist | Missing copy = discrepancy |
| **Third-party docs** | Usually acceptable unless expressly prohibited | May require beneficiary reference inclusion |
| **Partial shipments** | Must check: allowed or prohibited | Sending partial when prohibited |
| **Transshipment** | Must check: allowed or prohibited | Important for Tianjin → transshipment at Hong Kong/Singapore |

### 4.3 Typical L/C Discrepancy Costs

| Discrepancy | Bank Charge | Potential Penalty |
|-------------|:----------:|:-----------------:|
| Late shipment | CHF 50–150 per discrepancy | Rejection of documents |
| Missing document | CHF 50–150 per discrepancy | Buyer may refuse payment |
| Description mismatch | CHF 50–150 per discrepancy | Waiver required from buyer |
| Weight deviation >5% | CHF 50–150 per discrepancy | Retrading or refusal |

## 5. Incoterms 2020 for Potato Flake Shipments

| Incoterm | Shipper Responsibility | Risk Transfer Point | Recommended For |
|----------|----------------------|---------------------|-----------------|
| **EXW** (Ex Works) | Make goods available at factory | Buyer's premises | Domestic shipments only |
| **FOB** Tianjin/Qingdao/Shanghai | Load goods on vessel | On board vessel | Bulk shipments, experienced buyers |
| **CFR** (Cost & Freight) | FOB + ocean freight | On board vessel | Standard for most international sales |
| **CIF** (Cost, Insurance & Freight) | CFR + marine insurance | On board vessel | Buyers in developing markets |
| **DAP** / **DDP** | Full door-to-door logistics | At destination | Premium buyers, full-service model |

## 7. Common Documentation Pitfalls and Solutions

| Issue | Consequence | Prevention |
|-------|-------------|------------|
| CIQ certificate validity expires before vessel arrival | Cargo may be held for re-inspection | Schedule CIQ inspection within 3–5 days of loading |
| Phytosanitary certificate doesn't mention specific pest list | Rejection by importing country customs | Verify importing country's specific requirements before application |
| Commercial invoice and B/L weight mismatch | L/C discrepancy or customs hold | Double-check all weight figures before document issuance |
| Wood pallets without ISPM 15 marking | Rejection at destination; fumigation on arrival | Use HT-marked pallets only |
| L/C document list incomplete | Discrepancy — deduction CHF 50–150 each | Develop a pre-presentation checklist matched to L/C terms |
| Country of origin rule not verified for FTA preference | Denied preferential tariff treatment | Verify product meets the specific ROO (e.g., regional value content ≥40%) |

## 8. Digitalization and E-Document Trends

The global trade documentation landscape is modernizing rapidly:

| Platform/Initiative | Scope | Impact on Potato Flake Trade |
|--------------------|-------|------------------------------|
| **e-B/L (electronic B/L)** | Electronic transfer of title documents | Reduces document transit from 2 weeks to seconds |
| **China International Trade Single Window** | One-stop digital customs clearance | Faster CIQ document issuance (1–3 hours vs 1–2 days) |
| **Blockchain trade finance** | Digital L/C and smart contracts | Reduced discrepancy processing time |
| **EU ICS2** | Import Control System 2 for security | Additional data elements required for EU-bound cargo |
| **WCO Data Model** | Standardized electronic customs messaging | Long-term convergence of document formats |
