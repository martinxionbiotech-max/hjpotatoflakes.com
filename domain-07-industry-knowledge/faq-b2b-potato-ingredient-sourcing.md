### Q2: What is the difference between potato flakes and potato powder?

| Parameter | Potato Flakes | Potato Powder |
|-----------|:-------------:|:-------------:|
| **Particle form** | Thin, irregular flakes (typically 1–5 mm) | Fine, granular powder (80–200 mesh) |
| **Typical particle size** | ≥95% through 850 μm (20 mesh) | ≥90% through 150 μm (100 mesh) |
| **Reconstitution behavior** | Quick rehydration, fluffy mash | Requires mixing, can be lumpy if not agglomerated |
| **Best application** | Mashed potatoes, dough conditioning | Dry mixes, soups, batters, coatings |
| **Bulk density** | 350–450 g/L | 500–700 g/L |
| **Shelf life** | 12–24 months (standard packaging) | 18–24 months (standard packaging) |

> **Tip:** Hongji Agriculture offers both product forms from the same integrated production line, ensuring consistent raw material quality between flake and powder products.

### Q4: What is the typical shelf life, and what factors affect it?

**Standard declared shelf life:**
- Standard packaging (PE-lined paper bags): **12 months** from production date
- Aluminum foil laminated packaging: **18–24 months**
- Vacuum/Modified Atmosphere packaging: **up to 24 months**

**Factors affecting shelf life:**
1. **Storage temperature** — ideal: 15–25°C; avoid >35°C (accelerates browning)
2. **Relative humidity** — storage environment must maintain <60% RH
3. **Packaging integrity** — any breach allows moisture ingress; moisture >8% triggers quality degradation
4. **Light exposure** — UV light accelerates lipid oxidation and discoloration
5. **Oxygen exposure** — headspace oxygen causes oxidative rancidity in residual lipids

## 3. Quality Control & Testing

### Q6: What quality testing do you perform on each production batch?

Each production batch undergoes the following standard tests:

**Physical / Chemical (every batch):**
- Moisture content (oven method, 105°C, 2h)
- Color measurement (Hunter L, a, b)
- Particle size distribution (sieve analysis)
- Bulk density (Scott volumeter method)
- pH of reconstituted product (6.0–7.0 typical)
- Sulfur dioxide (SO₂) residual
- Protein (Kjeldahl N×6.25)

**Microbiological (every batch or daily composite):**
- Aerobic Plate Count
- Coliforms + E. coli
- Salmonella (absence in 25g)
- Yeast & Mold
- Staphylococcus aureus
- Bacillus cereus

**Periodic / Third-party (quarterly or per customer request):**
- Heavy metals (ICP-MS: Pb, As, Cd, Hg)
- Pesticide residues (GC-MS/MS multi-residue scan, 200+ compounds)
- Acrylamide
- GMO analysis (PCR-based)
- Full nutritional panel

### Q7: How do you ensure food safety throughout production?

Hongji Agriculture operates a **HACCP-based food safety management system** covering:

1. **Raw material control** — CCP-1: incoming potato inspection for mold, rot, foreign objects
2. **Washing and peeling** — CCP-2: effective soil and pesticide removal verification
3. **Blanching** — CCP-3: time/temperature monitoring for PPO inactivation
4. **Drying** — CCP-4: drum temperature and residence time control for moisture ≤7.0%
5. **Metal detection** — CCP-5: ferrous, non-ferrous, and stainless steel detection after final packaging
6. **Allergen control** — Dedicated production lines; potential allergens (milk, soy, wheat) handled through separate systems

**Certifications held:**
- ISO 22000:2018 (Food Safety Management System)
- FSSC 22000 (GFSI-recognized)
- HACCP Certificate
- Halal Certificate (authorized by recognized Islamic body)
- Kosher Certificate (upon request)

### Q10: How do you handle non-conforming product or quality complaints?

Our quality complaint procedure follows these steps:

1. **Notification** — Buyer notifies within 7 days of receipt (14 days for hidden defects)
2. **Evidence collection** — Buyer provides photos, retained samples, and CoC (Certificate of Conformance)
3. **Joint investigation** — We review production batch records, HACCP logs, and retained samples
4. **Resolution options** (depending on findings):
   - Replacement shipment at our cost
   - Credit note for accepted non-conforming goods
   - Price adjustment for minor deviation
   - Return and refund (including freight)
5. **CAPA (Corrective and Preventive Action)** — Root cause analysis report within 30 days; updated SOPs if systemic issue identified

**Historical complaint rate:** <0.5% of shipments (based on 2024–2025 data)

### Q21: What is the best strategy for testing potato flakes in my application before committing to a full container?

**Recommended evaluation roadmap:**

**Stage 1 — Desk evaluation (Week 1)**
- Request full specification sheets (CoA format)
- Share your product's target application parameters (desired viscosity, color, particle size)
- Discuss formulation compatibility with our technical team

**Stage 2 — Sample testing (Week 2–3)**
- Request 1–2 kg free sample for lab-scale trials
- Test in your specific application (bakery, extruder, coating line, etc.)
- Compare side-by-side with current supplier using same formulation

**Stage 3 — Pilot order (Week 4–6)**
- Place a 2–5 MT trial order (LCL or consolidation)
- Run full-scale production test in your plant
- Evaluate on: processability, yield, organoleptic quality, consistency

**Stage 4 — Commercial order (Week 6–8)**
- Negotiate contract terms based on pilot results
- Order first FCL (26–28 MT)
- Establish regular quality monitoring protocol with agreed CoA parameters
