# Packaging & Logistics Guide for Potato Flakes & Potato Powder

**Document Type:** Technical Logistics Reference  
**Target Audience:** Supply chain managers, logistics coordinators, warehouse operators  
**Last Updated:** July 2026  
**Reference:** Hongji Agriculture (弘基农业) — [hjpotatoflakes.com](https://hjpotatoflakes.com)

---

## 1. Introduction

Proper packaging and logistics management is critical for maintaining the quality of dehydrated potato products throughout the global supply chain. Potato flakes and powder are hygroscopic, sensitive to temperature and humidity extremes, and vulnerable to mechanical damage, oxidation, and pest infestation. This guide provides comprehensive specifications for packaging materials, container loading configurations, sea freight conditions, and warehousing best practices.

## 2. Packaging Materials and Configurations

### 2.1 Primary Packaging Options

| Package Type | Material Composition | Barrier Properties | Best For | Cost Index |
|-------------|---------------------|:------------------:|----------|:----------:|
| **Standard PP Woven Bag** | Woven polypropylene + PE inner liner | Moderate moisture barrier; low oxygen barrier | Standard food-grade flakes; short-to-medium transit | 1.0 (baseline) |
| **Multi-wall Kraft Paper Bag** | 3–5 ply kraft paper + PE liner (Optional: FOIL inner) | Good moisture barrier; moderate oxygen barrier (with foil) | Premium products; retail presentation | 1.3–1.6 |
| **Aluminum Foil Laminated Bag** | PET/Al foil/PE multi-layer laminate | High moisture + oxygen barrier | Long shelf life; premium/high-value products | 2.0–2.5 |
| **BOPP / PET Laminated Woven Bag** | BOPP or PET film + woven PP + PE liner | Excellent moisture barrier; good oxygen barrier | Export shipments; humid destination markets | 1.5–1.8 |
| **FIBC (Flexible Intermediate Bulk Container)** | Woven PP with PE inner liner; optional baffles | Moderate barrier; can add foil inner | Large volume customers; semi-bulk handling | 0.4–0.6/kg |

### 2.2 Packaging Weight Standards

| Format | Common Net Weight | Tolerances | Pallet Configuration | Units per 20' GP |
|--------|:----------------:|:----------:|:--------------------:|:----------------:|
| Small bag | 5 kg, 10 kg | ±0.1 kg | 50–80 bags/pallet | 2,200–4,160 units |
| Standard bag | 25 kg | ±0.2 kg | 20 bags/pallet | 1,040 bags |
| Bulk bag | 500 kg | ±2 kg | 1–2 bags/pallet | 20 units |
| Jumbo bag | 1,000 kg | ±5 kg | 1 bag/pallet | 26 units (tight fit) |
| Octabin | 500–1,000 kg | ±3 kg | 1 unit/pallet | 20 units |

### 2.3 Inner Liner Specifications

| Liner Type | Thickness | Material | Moisture Vapor Transmission Rate (MVTR) | Best For |
|------------|:---------:|:--------:|:---------------------------------------:|----------|
| Standard PE | 0.06–0.08 mm | LDPE | ≤5 g/m²/day | Short transit (<30 days) |
| Heavy PE | 0.10–0.15 mm | LDPE/HDPE | ≤3 g/m²/day | Standard transit (30–60 days) |
| Foil laminate | 0.12–0.18 mm | AL/PE | ≤0.5 g/m²/day | Long transit (>60 days) + tropical conditions |
| Vacuum-sealable | 0.15–0.20 mm | Nylon/PE | ≤0.2 g/m²/day | Premium long-shelf-life products |

### 2.4 Sealing Methods

| Method | Equipment Required | Seal Integrity | Speed | Suitability |
|--------|-------------------|:--------------:|:----:|------------|
| Heat seal (impulse) | Hand or automatic heat sealer | Good | Medium | PE liners, standard bags |
| Heat seal (continuous band) | Belt-driven continuous sealer | Excellent | High | Production line packaging |
| Ultrasonic seal | Ultrasonic bag sealer | Excellent | High | Foil/laminate bags |
| Sewn closure + tape | Bag sewing machine + sealing tape | Good | High | PP woven bags (without inner liner) |
| Pinch-bottom seal | Pre-applied hot-melt adhesive | Excellent | High | Multi-wall kraft bags |

> **Recommendation:** For export shipments exceeding 30 days' sea transit, use heat-sealed liners with a vacuum or nitrogen flush to reduce headspace oxygen.

## 3. Container Loading Specifications

### 3.1 Container Selection

| Container Type | Internal Dimensions (L×W×H, cm) | Max Payload (kg) | Typical Potato Flake Loading (MT) |
|---------------|:-----------------------------:|:----------------:|:----------------------------------:|
| 20' GP (General Purpose) | 590 × 235 × 239 | 28,200 | 26–28 |
| 40' GP | 1,203 × 235 × 239 | 28,600 | 26–28* |
| 40' HC (High Cube) | 1,203 × 235 × 269 | 28,800 | Volume-limited; ~26–28 |
| 20' OT (Open Top) | 590 × 235 × 239 | 28,000 | Jumbo bag loading |

*\*40' containers are weight-limited rather than volume-limited for bulk potato flakes (density ~350–450 g/L) — you fill the container weight before reaching full volume.*

### 3.2 Stowage Plan for 25 kg Bags in a 20' GP Container

```
Side View (lengthwise):
┌──────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ ↑ 7 layers of bags                                    │
│ │   1 pallet = 20 bags (5×4 pattern)                  │
│ │   Pallet height: 14cm × 7 + 15cm pallet = ~113cm    │
│ │                                                      │
│ │   Total: 20 pallets per 20' GP (2 rows × 10 deep)   │
│ │   = 400 bags = 10,000 kg (10 MT)                     │
│ └──────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

Alternative: Floor-loaded loose bags (no pallets):
  26–28 MT possible (1,040–1,120 bags)
  Requires manual loading/unloading
```

### 3.3 Loading Configuration Comparison

| Method | Net Weight (20' GP) | Loading Time | Unloading Method | Damage Risk | Best For |
|--------|:------------------:|:------------:|:----------------:|:-----------:|----------|
| **Palletized** | 10–12 MT (limited by pallet count) | 1–2 hours | Forklift | Low | LCL/partial; mechanized ports |
| **Floor-loaded (hand-stacked)** | 26–28 MT (full weight capacity) | 4–6 hours | Manual | Medium | FCL; manual unloading available |
| **Slip-sheet (no pallet)** | 24–26 MT | 2–3 hours | Push-pull forklift attachment | Low-Medium | Reduced wood packaging cost |
| **Jumbo bags (500 kg)** | 10 MT (20 units) | 1–2 hours | Forklift hoist | Low | Semi-bulk handling |
| **Jumbo bags (1,000 kg)** | 26 MT (26 units) | 2–3 hours | Forklift hoist | Low | High-volume FCL |

### 3.4 Container Preparation Checklist

Prior to loading, the container must be inspected for:

| Item | Standard Requirement | Inspection Method |
|------|---------------------|------------------|
| Cleanliness | No residue, odor, or previous cargo dust | Visual + olfactory check |
| Dryness | No moisture, condensation, or standing water | Touch test + moisture meter |
| Floor integrity | No holes, cracks, or protruding nails | Visual + light check |
| Door seals | Complete, flexible, no gaps | Visual + close test |
| Ventilation | Ventilator covers open/closing functional | Manual check |
| Interior temperature | <30°C for loading | Infrared thermometer |
| Odor check | No chemical, paint, or mold odor | Olfactory |

> **⛔ Critical:** Never load potato flakes into a container that has previously carried aromatic chemicals, rubber, leather, or animal products without thorough deodorization and professional cleaning.

## 4. Sea Freight Conditions

### 4.1 Recommended Maritime Shipping Conditions

| Parameter | Recommended Range | Critical Limits | Consequences of Non-Compliance |
|-----------|:-----------------:|:---------------:|--------------------------------|
| Container temperature | 15–25°C | <5°C or >40°C | Condensation damage (cold); accelerated browning (heat) |
| Relative humidity | <60% | >75% | Moisture ingress, clumping, mold growth |
| Ventilation | Closed during sea transit | Check after loading | Minimizes moisture exchange with ambient air |
| Stowage location | Below deck, away from engine room | Not on deck | Avoid temperature extremes; avoid heat source |
| Transit time (standard shipping routes) | See below | — | — |

### 4.3 Climate Zone Packaging Recommendations

| Climate Zone | Transit Season | Risk Factors | Recommended Packaging |
|--------------|---------------|--------------|----------------------|
| **Temperate** (Europe, North America) | Year-round | Moderate humidity | Standard 0.08mm PE liner |
| **Tropical** (SE Asia, Africa) | Monsoon season | High humidity, rain | 0.12mm PE + desiccant |

| **Cold climate** (Russia, Nordic) | Winter | Condensation on warming | Slow warming protocol at destination |
| **Cross-equator** | Any | Humidity + temperature swing | Foil or heavy PE + desiccant |

## 5. Warehouse & Storage Conditions

### 5.1 Recommended Warehouse Specifications

| Parameter | Standard | Premium / Long-term |
|-----------|:-------:|:-------------------:|
| Temperature | 15–25°C | 18–22°C |
| Relative humidity | <60% RH | <50% RH |
| Dew point management | Container stored at ≥10°C below ambient | Climate-controlled warehouse |
| Lighting | No direct sunlight; UV-filtered lighting | Dark storage preferred |
| Ventilation | Adequate air circulation; no stagnant zones | Positive pressure filtered air |
| Flooring | Smooth concrete; sealed; dust-free | Epoxy-coated; washable |
| Pest control | IPM program; insect monitors every 10m | Continuous monitoring + automatic traps |
| Stacking height | ≤6 bags (standard); ≤2 FIBC | ≤6 bags; only first-in-first-out floored |

### 5.2 FIFO (First-In, First-Out) Requirements

Warehousing must operate a strict **FIFO inventory management** system:

```
Storage Rack Layout:
┌──────────┐  ┌──────────┐  ┌──────────┐
│ Batch A  │  │ Batch B  │  │ Batch C  │  ← Incoming
│ (oldest) │→│          │→│  (newest)│
└──────────┘  └──────────┘  └──────────┘
                                   ↓
                            Outgoing (always oldest first)
```

- Each pallet/slot must be labeled with: batch number, production date, quantity, and receipt date.
- Digital WMS (Warehouse Management System) integration recommended.

### 5.3 Storage Life by Packaging and Conditions

| Packaging | Storage Condition | Guaranteed Shelf Life | Extended Shelf Life (proven) |
|-----------|-----------------|:--------------------:|:---------------------------:|
| Standard PP/PE, 25 kg | 15–25°C, <60% RH | 12 months | 18 months (proven stability) |
| Foil laminate, 25 kg | 15–25°C, <60% RH | 18 months | 24 months |
| Vacuum + foil, 25 kg | 15–25°C, <60% RH | 24 months | 30+ months |
| FIBC, standard PE | 15–25°C, <60% RH | 9 months | 12 months |

## 6. Desiccant and Oxygen Control

### 6.1 Desiccant Requirements for Container Shipments

For shipments crossing high-humidity or tropical zones:

| Container Type | Desiccant Type | Quantity Recommended | Placement |
|---------------|---------------|:-------------------:|-----------|
| 20' GP (palletized) | Silica gel (unit pack) | 1–2 kg total (10–20 units × 100g) | Suspend from ceiling hooks |
| 20' GP (floor loaded) | Silica gel (unit pack) | 2–3 kg total | Layered between bags + ceiling |
| 40' GP/HC | Silica gel (unit pack) | 3–5 kg total | Distributed along container length |
| 20' GP (tropical transit) | Calcium chloride + silica combo | 4–6 kg total | Heavy dehumidification needed |

### 6.2 Oxygen Control Strategies

| Method | Oxygen Reduction | Cost Impact | Application |
|--------|:---------------:|:-----------:|-------------|
| Nitrogen flushing (bag level) | <2% O₂ residual | +$2–5/bag | Premium bags, long shelf life |
| Oxygen absorber sachets | <0.1% O₂ residual | +$0.3–1.0/bag | Small packs, consumer products |
| Vacuum packaging | Near-zero O₂ | +$1–3/bag | High-value organic products |
| Modified atmosphere (MAP) | 2–5% O₂ residual | +$0.5–2/bag | Standard improvement |

## 7. Handling Precautions

### 7.1 Loading / Unloading Safety

| Step | Procedure | Critical Points |
|------|-----------|-----------------|
| Lifting | Use forklift with appropriate capacity | Never exceed pallet weight rating |
| Stacking | Interlock pattern; max 6 bags high | Center of gravity; bag deformity check |
| Container loading | Stack from front to back; block and brace | No gaps; strap or inflatable dunnage for bracing |
| Receiving | Weigh each pallet; inspect for damage | Reject any water-damaged or torn packaging |
| Sampling | Use sanitized trier/probe | Sample after surface layer removal |

### 7.2 Damage Classification and Disposition

| Damage Type | Severity | Action |
|-------------|:--------:|--------|
| Torn outer bag, intact inner liner | Minor | Repair with packaging tape; use first |
| Torn inner liner, product exposed | Moderate | Repack immediately; test for moisture |
| Water-damaged bag | Critical | Isolate; test moisture + microbiology |
| Crushed/dented | Minor-Moderate | Check for integrity; segregate if deformed |
| Pest evidence (insects, droppings) | Critical | Quarantine entire pallet; professional inspection |
| Off-odor / chemical taint | Critical | Reject entire affected lot |

## 8. Temperature Excursion Management

### 8.1 Temperature Tolerances During Transit

| Temperature Range | Duration | Product Impact | Action |
|:-----------------:|:--------:|---------------|--------|
| 15–25°C | Unlimited | No significant impact | Normal handling |
| 25–30°C | <30 days | Minimal; slight color darkening possible | Accept; test color upon arrival |
| 30–35°C | <14 days | Noticeable browning; 5–8% L-value reduction | Test carefully; discount consideration |
| 35–40°C | >7 days | Significant quality loss; accelerated Maillard | May reject based on specification |
| >40°C | Any | Probable severe quality degradation | Reject; file insurance claim |
| <5°C (with condensation) | On arrival warming | Condensation risk; moisture migration | Slow warming protocol (see 8.2) |

### 8.2 Slow Warming Protocol for Cold-Transit Shipments

When containers arrive below 10°C in a warm climate (e.g., winter shipment arriving at tropical port):

1. **Do not open container doors** immediately
2. **Acclimate** for 12–24 hours in shaded, ventilated area
3. **Monitor** internal container temperature rise
4. When interior reaches within 5°C of ambient: open doors partially
5. Wait 2–4 hours before full unloading
6. **Inspect** bags for condensation; wipe dry before warehousing

## 9. Insurance and Claims

### 9.1 Recommended Insurance Coverage

| Insurance Type | Coverage | Premium Range |
|---------------|----------|:------------:|
| ICC (A) — Institute Cargo Clauses (All Risks) | All physical loss/damage | 0.15–0.25% of cargo value |
| ICC (B) — With Average | Partial loss with named perils | 0.10–0.18% |
| ICC (C) — Free of Particular Average | Total loss only (limited) | 0.06–0.12% |
| Institute War Clauses | War risks | Additional 0.02–0.05% |
| Institute Strikes Clauses | Strike, riot, civil commotion | Additional 0.01–0.03% |

> **Recommendation:** For potato flake shipments, always insure at **ICC (A)** — All Risks, at 110% of CIF invoice value.

### 9.2 Common Claim Scenarios

| Damage Type | Evidence Required | Typical Claim Recovery |
|-------------|-------------------|:---------------------:|
| Water damage from seawater | Photographs, survey report, B/L | 70–100% (depending on policy) |
| Condensation damage | Survey report, temperature log | 50–80% (negotiable) |
| Infestation | Pest identification report | 80–100% |
| Physical damage (crushing) | Loading photos, unpacking video | 50–100% |
| Short weight | Weighbridge ticket, tally sheet | 100% of shortfall |

## 10. Regulatory Compliance for Packaging Materials

### 10.1 Food Contact Material Regulations by Destination

| Market | Regulation | Key Requirements |
|--------|-----------|------------------|
| **China** | GB 4806.1-2016, GB 9685-2016 | General safety; migration limits for plastic materials |
| **European Union** | EU Reg 10/2011 (Plastics), EC 1935/2004 | Overall migration limit ≤10 mg/dm²; specific migration limits for monomers |
| **United States** | 21 CFR 170-199 (Indirect Food Additives) | GRAS or regulated food contact substances |
| **Japan** | Food Sanitation Law (1959), MHLW Notification | Positive list system; strict heavy metal limits |
| **Australia/New Zealand** | FSANZ Standard 1.4.1 | Compliance with EU or US standards accepted |

### 10.2 ISPM 15 Compliance for Wood Packaging

All wood packaging materials (pallets, dunnage, crating) must be:

- **Heat treated** to 56°C core temperature for 30 minutes minimum
- **Marked** with IPPC logo + country code + treatment facility code + HT (or MB for methyl bromide)
- **Free of bark** (EU regulation requires bark-free wood packaging since 2021)

## 11. Summary Checklist for Dispatch

Before shipment, verify the following:

1. ☐ Packaging matches buyer specification (material, weight, sealing)
2. ☐ Inner liner integrity confirmed (air-leak test on random samples)
3. ☐ Pallet type meets ISPM 15 requirements (if wood)
4. ☐ Container inspected (clean, dry, odor-free, floor intact)
5. ☐ Container temperature ≤30°C at loading
6. ☐ Desiccant placed (if transiting tropical/high-humidity zone)
7. ☐ Weight verified (VGM certificate obtained)
8. ☐ Container sealed (seal number recorded + photographed)
9. ☐ Shipping mark applied both sides of container
10. ☐ Container stowage plan shared with shipping line
