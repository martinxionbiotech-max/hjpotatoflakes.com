## 1. Introduction

Exporting potato flakes and potato powder to international markets requires rigorous compliance with an increasingly complex web of food safety regulations, labeling laws, contaminant limits, and import control systems. Non-compliance can result in shipment rejection, detention at port, inclusion on import alert lists, and reputational damage.

This document provides a structured reference for the key regulatory frameworks governing potato-based ingredients in the five primary export destination blocs: the United States (FDA), European Union, Australia/New Zealand (FSANZ), Japan, and other important markets. For each jurisdiction, we outline product-specific requirements, permissible additives, contaminant limits, labeling mandates, and import clearance procedures.

## 3. United States — FDA Compliance

### 3.1 Applicable Regulations

| Regulation | Scope | Key Provisions for Potato Flakes |
|------------|-------|---------------------------------|
| **21 CFR Part 110** (CGMP in Manufacturing, Packing, or Holding Human Food) | Current GMP requirements | Personnel hygiene, plant sanitation, equipment maintenance, process controls |
| **21 CFR Part 117** (FSMA Preventive Controls) | Hazard analysis & risk-based preventive controls | Requires written food safety plan, hazard analysis, preventive controls, supply-chain program |
| **21 CFR Part 101** (Food Labeling) | Labeling of food products | Nutrition Facts panel format, ingredient declaration, allergen labeling (FALCPA) |
| **21 CFR Part 165** (Beverages — incidental) | General requirements for standardized foods | Reference for dehydrated product definitions |
| **21 CFR Part 170-199** | Food additives & GRAS | Permitted additives for dehydrated potatoes |
| **FSMA — Foreign Supplier Verification Program (FSVP)** | Importer obligation to verify foreign suppliers | Hongji as foreign supplier must share food safety documentation with US importer |
| **FDA Prior Notice** (21 CFR 1.276-1.285) | Advance notification of imported food shipments | Must be filed ≥2 hours before air arrival; ≥8 hours before truck/rail; ≥24 hours before vessel |

### 3.2 Contaminant Action Levels (FDA Compliance Policy Guide)

| Contaminant | Action Level | Reference Document |
|-------------|:-----------:|-------------------|
| **Lead (Pb)** | ≤0.5 mg/kg (as consumed basis) | CPG Sec 545.400 |
| **Arsenic (As)** | ≤0.5 mg/kg (inorganic) | CPG Sec 575.100 |
| **Cadmium (Cd)** | ≤0.2 mg/kg | CPG Sec 545.300 |
| **Mercury (Hg)** | ≤0.02 mg/kg (total) | CPG Sec 540.200 |
| **Aflatoxins (B1+B2+G1+G2)** | ≤20 μg/kg (total) | CPG Sec 555.400 |
| **Pesticide residues** | Per 40 CFR 180 tolerance levels | MRL database |
| **Sulfur dioxide** | ≤50 mg/kg (GRAS limit) | 21 CFR 182.3637 |

### 3.4 FDA Import Process

```
Step 1: FDA Facility Registration (renewed every even-numbered year by Dec 31)
         → Hongji must have valid facility registration number
         
Step 2: FSVP compliance
         → US importer must have FSVP compliant program
         → Hongji must provide FSVP documentation to importer (CoA, HACCP, supplier audits)
         
Step 3: Prior Notice submission (by US importer or agent)
         → Submitted via FDA PNSI system
         → Requires: product code, quantity, manufacturer, shipper, consignee
         
Step 4: Customs clearance + FDA review
         → FDA may issue "Notice of FDA Action" within 24 hours of Prior Notice
         → May require physical sampling or documentation review
         
Step 5: Release or detention
         → Green: Release without examination
         → Yellow: May be examined without further notice
         → Red: Refused entry; product must be re-exported or destroyed
```

### 3.5 Detention Without Physical Examination (DWPE)

Products may be placed on FDA's Import Alert (DWPE) if:

- History of Salmonella positives in potato products
- History of sulfite violations (undeclared or exceeding limits)
- History of pesticide residue violations
- FSVP non-compliance by US importer

**Removal from DWPE:** Submit 5 subsequent clean shipment samples (per FDA protocol), demonstrate corrective actions, and file a petition.

| Glyphosate | 0.50 | — |
| Mancozeb (dithiocarbamates) | 5.0 | — |

| Additive | Permitted in EU? | Maximum Level (EU) | Notes |
|----------|:--------------:|:------------------:|-------|
| TBHQ | **✗ Not permitted** | N/A | Banned in dehydrated potato products |
| BHA | **✗ Not permitted** | N/A | Banned in dehydrated potato products |
| BHT | **✗ Not permitted** | N/A | Banned in dehydrated potato products |
| Sulfur dioxide | **✓ Permitted** | 50 mg/kg (as SO₂) | Must be declared on label if >10 mg/kg |
| SAPP | **✓ Permitted** | 2,500 mg/kg (as diphosphate) | Additive E450 |
| Ascorbic acid (E300) | ✓ GMP | Quantum satis | Allowed without restriction |

### 4.5 EU Allergen Declaration (14 Mandatory Allergens)

Under Regulation (EU) 1169/2011, the following 14 allergens must be highlighted (bold, italic, or contrasting color) if present or used in processing:

1. Cereals containing gluten (wheat, rye, barley, oats, spelt, kamut)
2. Crustaceans
3. Eggs
4. Fish
5. Peanuts
6. Soybeans
7. Milk (including lactose)
8. Nuts (almonds, hazelnuts, walnuts, cashews, pecans, etc.)
9. Celery
10. Mustard
11. Sesame seeds
12. Sulfur dioxide and sulfites (>10 mg/kg)
13. Lupin
14. Molluscs

> **For potato flakes:** Typically only **sulfites (≥10 mg/kg)** are relevant. If produced on shared lines with soy, wheat, or milk, cross-contamination must be assessed through risk analysis and may require advisory labeling.

### 4.6 EU Import Process

1. **Trader registration** — EU importer must be registered as a Food Business Operator (FBO)
2. **Health certificate** — Model health certificate for composite products may be required
3. **Laboratory testing** — On-arrival testing frequency based on origin risk category
4. **Customs clearance** — Import control system (ICS2) security filing
5. **Approved establishment** — Exporting facility may need EU approval (Decision 2020/2151 for certain animal origin; not strictly required for plant-based products, but recommended)

## 5. Australia & New Zealand — FSANZ Compliance

### 5.1 Applicable Standards

| Standard | Scope |
|----------|-------|
| **FSANZ Food Standards Code** | Comprehensive food regulatory framework |
| **Standard 1.1.1 — Structure** | Preliminary provisions and application |
| **Standard 1.2.1 — Labeling** | General labeling requirements |
| **Standard 1.2.3 — Mandatory Warning Statements** | Warning statements and advisory labels |
| **Standard 1.3.1 — Food Additives** | Permitted additives (Schedule 14, 15, 16) |
| **Standard 1.4.1 — Contaminants & Natural Toxicants** | Heavy metals, mycotoxins, etc. |
| **Standard 1.4.2 — Maximum Residue Limits** | Agricultural and veterinary chemicals |
| **Standard 2.10.1 — General provisions for fruit, vegetables** | Covers dehydrated plant products |

### 5.2 Contaminant Limits (FSANZ Standard 1.4.1)

| Contaminant | Maximum Level (mg/kg) | Notes |
|-------------|:--------------------:|-------|
| Lead (Pb) | 0.5 | Applies to processed fruit/vegetable products |
| Cadmium (Cd) | 0.2 | |
| Arsenic (As) | 1.0 (total) | Inorganic limit: 0.5 mg/kg where specified |
| Mercury (Hg) | 0.05 | |
| Tin (Sn) | 250 | Canned products only |
| Patulin | Not specified | More relevant to apples |

### 5.3 Unique FSANZ Requirements for Import

1. **Biosecurity Import Conditions (BICON)** — Australian Department of Agriculture requires:
   - Phytosanitary certificate for potato-derived products
   - Proof of processing (heat treatment) to eliminate pest risk
   - May require import permit (BICON assessment)

2. **Permitted Additives** — FSANZ adopts many Codex standards but maintains a separate positive list:
   - Sulfur dioxide: ≤50 mg/kg (as consumed)
   - SAPP: ≤2,500 mg/kg
   - TBHQ, BHA, BHT: Permitted at ≤200 mg/kg each (combined ≤200 mg/kg)

3. **Quarantine Considerations** — Australia has strict quarantine rules:
   - Potatoes are a high-risk commodity for pathogen introduction
   - Must confirm processing conditions (temperature/time) eliminate viability
   - **Synchytrium endobioticum** (potato wart) and **Ralstonia solanacearum** (brown rot) are zero-tolerance quarantine pests

### 5.4 Import Clearance Process (Australia)

```
Step 1: BICON assessment — Determine if import permit required
Step 2: Biosecurity Import Permit (if applicable) — Valid period: 2 years
Step 3: Phytosanitary certificate from GAC China
Step 4: Department of Agriculture border inspection
         → Document check + may include visual or physical inspection
Step 5: Post-border monitoring (if deemed high risk)
Step 6: Release for sale — Subject to meeting FSANZ labeling requirements
```

## 6. Japan — Food Sanitation Law Compliance

### 6.1 Applicable Legislation

| Legislation | Scope | Key Requirements |
|-------------|-------|------------------|
| **Food Sanitation Act (Law No. 233, 1947)** | General food safety | Comprehensive regulation covering all processed foods |
| **Food Labeling Act (Act No. 70, 2015)** | Labeling requirements | Replaced previous JAS labeling system |
| **MHLW Notification** | Additives, contaminants, pesticide MRLs | Ministerial notifications under the Food Sanitation Act |
| **Positive List System for Pesticides** | Pesticide residues | MRLs established for ~650 pesticides; ≤0.01 mg/kg for unlisted pesticides |
| **Plant Protection Law** | Quarantine pests | Quarantine stations at all ports of entry |

### 6.2 Additives Permitted in Japan (MHLW Positive List)

| Additive | Status | Maximum Level |
|----------|:-----:|:-------------:|
| Sulfur dioxide | Permitted | ≤30 mg/kg (stricter than US/EU's 50 mg/kg) |
| Sodium acid pyrophosphate | Permitted | GMP |
| Ascorbic acid | Permitted | GMP |
| Citric acid | Permitted | GMP |
| Tocopherols (Vitamin E) | Permitted | GMP |
| **TBHQ** | **✗ Not permitted** | N/A |
| **BHA** | **✗ Not permitted** | N/A |
| **BHT** | **✗ Not permitted** | N/A |

> 🇯🇵 **Japan-specific:** Sulfur dioxide limit is reduced to **30 mg/kg** (vs 50 mg/kg in most other markets). Formulation must be adjusted accordingly. Also, TBHQ, BHA, and BHT are not permitted — similar to EU regulation.

### 6.3 Pesticide Residue Limits (Japan Positive List System)

| Property | Standard |
|----------|---------|
| Number of listed pesticides | ~650 with established MRLs |
| Default tolerance for unlisted pesticides | **0.01 mg/kg** (uniform limit) |
| Prohibited pesticides | All unregistered/unlisted pesticides subject to zero-tolerance |
| Testing requirement | Multi-residue GC-MS/MS + LC-MS/MS screening covering 500+ compounds |
| Administrative penalties | Shipment rejected; exporter may be placed on administrative guidance list |

### 6.4 Japan Import Procedures

1. **Import Notification** — Must be filed via NACCS (Nippon Automated Cargo and Port Consolidated System)
2. **Inspection at quarantine station**:
   - Plant quarantine: Inspection for live pests/diseases
   - Food sanitation inspection: Sampling for additives, pesticide residues, microbiology
3. **Certificate of Analysis (Japanese translation required)**:
   - Must include specific parameters expected by Japanese importers
   - Heavy metals (Pb, As, Cd, Hg)
   - Residual SO₂
   - Microbiological testing
4. **Labeling under Food Labeling Act**:
   - Product name (Japanese)
   - Raw material list (Japanese)
   - Allergens (specific to JFA 28-item list)
   - Net content
   - "Best before" date (年月日 format)
   - Importer name and address (Japan)
   - Country of origin: "原料原産地名: 中国"
   - Recommended: add "Product of China" in English + Japanese

## 9. Traceability and Recall Preparedness

### 9.1 One-Step-Forward / One-Step-Back Traceability

The Codex and all major regulatory frameworks require:

| Step | Record | Retention Period |
|------|--------|:---------------:|
| **One-step back** (incoming) | Supplier name, delivery date, lot number, quantity, CoA | ≥5 years (EU: ≥7 years for some documents) |
| **Internal traceability** | Processing batch records, blend ratios, CIP logs | ≥3 years or product shelf life +1 year |
| **One-step forward** (outgoing) | Customer name, shipment date, B/L number, quantity | ≥5 years |

### 9.2 Mock Recall Requirements

| Certification | Frequency | Acceptance Criteria |
|--------------|:---------:|:------------------:|
| BRCGS Issue 9 | At least annually | 100% trace from finished product to raw material within 4 hours |
| FSSC 22000 | At least annually | Full trace ≤4 hours; ≥95% recovery within stated target |
| SQF Edition 9 | At least annually | ≤2 hours to demonstrate full traceability |
